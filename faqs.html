FAQs

What is a Quaker wedding like?

Quaker weddings are a special type of wedding where there is no officiant, and the couple chooses to self-unite, witnessed by friends and family.

The wedding will take place in Quaker silent worship, with everyone sitting in silence. The couple will rise and exchange “declarations” with each other. As the Meeting continues, we will continue to sit in silence, with folks occasionally rising out of the silence to share a message. Listen to what comes from your heart that day, if you want to say a few words. It can be brief message, such as an endearing story, a favorite poem or song, or advice. It should be in the moment (i.e. not pre-rehearsed), and should not be like a toast. There is no obligation to say anything. Choosing to not say something is just as welcome. In between messages, we will sit in silence for reflection.

At the close of the hour, everyone will sign the marriage certificate as witnesses, and the couple will be legally wed!

Are children welcome?

Yes, absolutely! Please note that the Quaker ceremony will be approximately an hour of sitting in mostly silence. There will be other areas open in Arch Street if the kids need a break.

Yes! Please indicate in the RSVP form any dietary restrictions, as well as allergies. The restaurant will be able to accomodate allergies and contamination safety.

Please dress semi-formal (ex: suit or jacket with tie, dress, or skirt) but comfortably. It will be hot in Philadelphia in August!
