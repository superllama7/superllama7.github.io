About Us

Kenji and Brett met on New Year’s Day in 2020 over a delicious snack of homemade apple galette (a Kenji delicacy) and discussions of the Golden Record and Pina Bausch.
When I met him, I thought Brett was such a sweet, shy boy. A good listener. Curious about random topics, the city, and the world around him.
Kenji
Kenji’s endless array of facts and anecdotes related to almost anything (based on years of listening to 99% Invisible, amongst other research for fun) and his curiosity concerning things he didn’t know about felt so invigorating and led me wanting to explore my own world of interests more fully.
Brett
A few weeks into knowing each other, we took the Staten Island Ferry on a cold winter day to Staten Island, to go to the National Lighthouse Museum. Afterwards, we got Sri Lanken food at a tiny restaurant with the sweetest woman (shoutout to Viji at New Asha), who said ‘don’t worry about the menu. I’ll pick the food and take good care of you.’ Hanging out with Brett that whole day felt so easy and fun. I had a big crush on him.
Kenji
Brett and Kenji accidentally took the next step in their relationship in March 2020 when the COVID-19 pandemic forced their hand and they moved in with each other.

Despite the scariness and unknown of that time, and never having any alone time from each other, it was really lovely and even fun. We cooked good food, watched a lot of movies, and gave each other bad haircuts.

Kenji

While this leap could have been disastrous, we were immediately compatible (even in a NYC studio apartment) and I knew from that moment this was real.

Brett
In 2021, Kenji had to move to LA for work. Brett had to stay in NYC for grad school.

Being long distance for three years was not only incredibly taxing (emotionally and financially), but a great opportunity for both of us to grow and consider what we wanted in the long term out of our relationship. Luckily I was a night owl and loved staying up to chat with Kenji every night after work or class. I also loved sending weekly emails to Kenji (Brett’s Magazine), sharing interesting articles/videos/songs I had come across throughout the week

Brett
After three years of long distance, Brett graduated from Columbia in May 2024 and took a cross-country Amtrak to move to LA.

Things I like and love about Kenji:

His interests both align and veer wildly from mine, introducing new hobbies into my life

His aptitude for baking

His endless empathy and kindness for others

His love for disco and ease on the dancefloor

His patience for dealing with my ongoing impressions (we are currently working through Nosferatu)

Brett

Brett makes me laugh more than anyone else. He’s stubborn, and has a good moral center. He’s incredibly empathetic, to me, and others. He’s always reading an article, always curious. He’s always down to watch a movie a second time with me.

Kenji

My relationship with Kenji has taught me the true meaning of security—feeling secure in myself and my relationship both with him and with others. I feel like I have been able to grow and better understand the person I want to be because of feeling secure around Kenji.

Brett

Brett brought out or emphasized a side of me - a silly, playful side. I feel stronger and braver being with him - I know that together we can do anything, and also feel so much braver by myself as well, knowing he’s supporting me.

Kenji
