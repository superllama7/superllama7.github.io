Arch Street Meeting House

Since 1682, the historic Arch Street Meeting House has been used by Quakers and the community. In 2011, the meeting house became a National Historic Landmark.

There is a large parking lot, approximately 70 spots, off 4th street. This will be open for day and overnight parking for the wedding. You may leave your car starting 4pm on Friday August 15 and must leave by Sunday August 17 at 10am. If you need extended parking, please check out Park America (231 N. 2nd St).

The full meeting house is handicap accessible.

FringeArts and Holiday Inn are a 12 minute walk away.

FringeArts

FringeArts and FringeBar share a historic building on the Delaware Waterfront, which used to be a high pressure pump house for the Philadelphia fire service.

The pump house was built in 1903 to help with challenge of fighting fires with regular pressure in a city growing upwards. The pump house pressurized water to 300 lbs and delivered it, 10,000 gallons of water a minute, to special red hydrants across the city, capable of shooting water 230 feet up in the air. For more history on the pump house, see Hidden City.

In 2013, the pump house was renovated into a performing arts space and restaurant, and is the home to the annual Philadelphia Fringe Festival.

The restaurant and theater are handicap accessible.

There is NO parking available on site, please park at Arch Street or Holiday Inn. Arch Street Meeting House is a 12 minute walk away, and Holiday Inn is 1 minute away.

Getting Here

By Bus / Train

Philadelphia is easily accessible by Amtrak or bus, if you are traveling along the East Coast.

If traveling from NYC, bus is the cheapest. MegaBus, Peter Pan, and Greyhound all run every couple of hours. The bus stop is at Spring Garden St and N Columbus Blvd, a 10 minute walk from FringeArts.

For Amtrak, the NE Regional runs through Philadelphia and stops in West Philly at 30th Street Station.

By Car

If driving by car, please park at Arch Street Meeting House, or, opt to pay for parking at the hotel ($37).

By Plane

Philadelphia International Airport is 12 miles from downtown. SEPTA regional trains depart from the airport every 30 minutes ($6.50), and take about 25 minutes to get to downtown Philadelphia. You can get off at 30th St Station, Suburban Station, or Jefferson Station. Jefferson Station is closest to Arch Street Meeting House, about a 17 minute walk. Or, at Jefferson Station, transfer to the Market Frankford Subway line at 11th Street Station, and take the subway to 2nd Street Station.

A taxi or ride share from the airport would cost about $30 (not including tip).

Things to Do

Not an exhaustive list by any means, but some of our favorites in the area. For more suggestions, see Uwishunu, TravelLemming, or GuideToPhilly.

Old City

Franklin Fountain - old-fashioned ice cream and sodas

Museum for Art in Wood - cute museum about wood art

Elfreth's Alley - the oldest continuously inhabited residental street in the country.

Headhouse Farmer's Market - Sundays 10a-2p

Center City

Liberty Bell - it's a big bell! If you don't want to wait in line, you can also get a glimpse of the bell looking through the windows from the outside of the center.

Independence Hall - known for the signing of the Declaration of Independence, U.S. Consitution, and National Treasure (2004)

Reading Terminal Market - a large bustling marketplace in an old railroad station, featuring 80 vendors.

Chinatown - good food and bakeries, as well as a great Vietnamese restaurant, Pho Xe Lua Viet, and Malaysian, Penang.

Middle Child - great breakfast sandwiches

Rittenhouse Square - lovely for a picnic or a stroll

Mutter Museum - medical history museums with anatomical and medical specimens.

Knead Bagels - fun flavors

Fairmount

Free Library of Philadelphia

Book Corner - supports the Free Library and has a cute cat

Philadelphia Museum of Art - historic art museum. You can also run up the Rocky steps or take a picture with the statue!

The Franklin Institute - family fun and science!

Barnes Foundation - incredible impressionist and post-impressionist collection in a striking building.

South Street and South Philly

South Philly Barbacoa - by Chef Cristina Martinez, made famous on Chef's Table

Philadelphia's Magic Garden - beautiful windy mosaics from Isaiah Zagar.

Bok - arts and shopping, built in an old high school. Machine Shop Bakery, Remark Glass, Bok Bar (rooftop bar)

John's Water Ice - cash only

Jim's South St - cheesesteaks, cash only

Philly AIDS Thrift - ginormous thrift shop

PHS Popup Garden at South St Beer Garden

Hardena - Indonensian restaurant

Other

Rita's Water Ice - many locations

Wawa - many locations

Sabrina's Cafe - multiple locations. Great brunch!

Wagner Free Institute of Science - rare example of a Victorian-era scientific society and collection. Huge mineral, animal, and insect specimen collections. Only open on weekdays.
